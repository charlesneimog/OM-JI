MODULATION-NOTES

[GENERIC-FUNCTION]
OM-JI
Function Reference
ARGUMENTS:
  - listnote First notelist of the comparation. [default = (6000 6530)]
  - listnote2 Second notelist of the comparation. [default = (7203 5049)]
  - cents Approximation in cents, in which the object will consider the notes as equal. For example, with the number 5, the object will consider 6000 and 6005 as equal notes. [default = 2]

Description:

Filter of notes that can be used to do the modulation between tuning regions. This idea appears in the reading of Daniel James Huey (2017) doctoral dissertation. He claims: 'This dissertation shows the importance of common tones between pitches of two tuning areas, and it highlights consonant intervals between the fundamental pitches of the tuning areas as a means of producing a sense of continuity.'
